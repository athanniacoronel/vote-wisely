WHAT IS THE SIGNIFICANCE OF VOTING?

Voting

It is the backbone of a democratic country. It is a process in which people elect their chosen leader in positions. With voting, the citizens voices are heard. It gives them the power to participate in the political process, since voting is not only a right but also a civil responsibility. Practicing their right to vote ensures that the candidates that are running into position are accountable. Voting enables the citizens to shape the future of their country. Since, the power of leading the country is in the politicians hands. It is a chance to make a difference and improvement so it is essential for people to vote for deserving candidates.

HOW TO AVOID ELECTION MISINFORMATION AND PROPAGANDA?

These tips can be a guide for voters to avoid being persuaded by misinformation and the propaganda of the candidate. This will not just give information to the voters but it'll practice their decision making skills.

  1. Check the credentials or background:

    It is a must to check their credentials or background to know their personal aspects, achievements, and qualities. This will allow the voters to know more about the candidates and in identifying the misinformation about them.

    For Example:
    • Educational Background
    • Work Experience
    • Previous Platforms
    • NBI clearance
  2. Seek reliable sources:

    Fact-checking is important to identify facts from misinformation. It is advisable to check in multiple articles or websites about the topic to further check if the information is credible.

  3. Educate yourself:

    Make sure to learn more about the candidate and their platforms from reliable sources. Take time to check if their platforms will be significant in the community. We must also be aware of our biases because it might affect our perception.

  4. Be a responsible netizen:

    We must be responsible for what we post and share online, as we might spread fake news instead of factual information.

  5. Be cautious:

    Many politicians use flowery words to make them appear deserving of your votes. We must always look out for sensational headlines and their propaganda.

STEPS TO ENSURE AN INFORMED DECISION BEFORE VOTING

  1. Introspect: Know Yourself and Your Values

    Before assessing candidates, take time to understand your core beliefs and priorities:

    • What values matter most to you? (e.g., honesty, integrity, patriotism, fairness)
    • What issues do you care about? (e.g., education, healthcare, economy, national security)
    • How do you define good leadership?
  2. Identify Your Non-Negotiables

    Establish clear deal-breakers that would prevent you from supporting a candidate, such as:

    • History of human rights violations
    • Corruption charges or involvement in scandals
    • Dishonesty or lack of transparency
    • Arrogance or unwillingness to listen
  3. Scrutinize the Candidates

    After knowing what you stand for, evaluate candidates based on their actions, not just their words:

    • Track Record: What have they actually accomplished in office or their profession?
    • Consistency: Have they been true to their principles, or do they change stances for political gain?
    • Leadership Style: Are they accountable, open to dialogue, and solution-oriented?
    • Decision-Making: Do they base decisions on research, expert advice, and public welfare?
  4. Research Policies & Compare Platforms
    • Read their official platforms and past speeches.
    • Compare their stances on key issues with other candidates.
    • Consider how their policies align with your values and the country’s needs.
  5. Evaluate Their Character & Public Service
    • How do they treat their opponents, critics, and the public?
    • Do they unite or divide people?
    • Have they demonstrated true public service, or are they driven by self-interest?
  6. Be Mindful of Propaganda & Misinformation
    • Verify claims using fact-checking organizations.
    • Avoid being swayed by emotional tactics, viral posts, or biased media.
    • Look beyond speeches and campaign promises—actions speak louder than words.
  7. Vote with Confidence

    By the time you cast your vote, you should feel confident that your choice aligns with your values, priorities, and vision for the future.
